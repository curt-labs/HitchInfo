12/13/2010, 10:46 AM Bike rack
Greg I have a Toyota Sienna with a Class III hitch (Curt). I have a bike rack and intend to carry 5 bikes with it (NorthShore Racks 6-bike model). With this rack the bikes are mounted vertically side to side, so each additional bike goes to the side, instead of further back. My hitch is rated for 350 lbs max tongue weight. The rack and 5 bikes will weight about 225 lbs. I will use airlift supports in the rear springs to level the ride. Does this sound safe? The rack manufacturer could not help with the hitch info. Rack url: http://www.northshoreracks.com/NSR%206-bike.htm
 
12/15/2010, 08:45 AM Re: Bike rack
Travis ~ Hitch Guy™ The largest factor here is the distance between the edge of the receiver tube to the furthest point of the bike rack. The 350lb tongue weight rating was tested using a ball mount about 3-5 inches from the edge of the receiver tube. The easiest way to determine your unsupported load capacity is to divide the tw rating of your hitch per foot extended. Please visit http://ow.ly/3pzRz to view a video we created that illustrates this equation.

Travis ~ Hitch Guy™ Greg, I think you're looking at a distance between 2.5 - 3ft. Which greatly limits your TW rating down to around 150lbs. If you were to use a stabilizing strap it would greatly decrease the added leverage of bouncing up and down while traveling. Click to http://ow.ly/3qMKI view the CURT 18050 stabilizing strap.
