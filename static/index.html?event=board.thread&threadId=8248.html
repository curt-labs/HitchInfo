11/16/2010, 04:36 PM Re: Towing Capacity
Travis ~ Hitch Guy™ Brandon, 8,000lbs actually is quite the load. 6,000lbs is the most commonly maximum for mid-90s 1/2 tons. You can't go wrong with a 95 or newer 3/4 ton truck or SUV. Dodge will be the most affordable. Although, even the 99 1500 Chevy Suburban has an 10,000lb rating. Suburbans are typically quite inexpensive due to older models getting terrible gas mileage. The older the vehicle the lesser the towing capacity. They didn't really start beefing up the frames and drive-trains for towing until the late 90's early 2000's. I know this isn't quite the answer you were looking for but it is the best advice I can offer.
