10/06/2010, 09:33 AM 4 pin flat or 6 pin round
Jackie We purchased a boat trailer which has a 6 pin round connector, our vehicle has a 4 pin flat connector. The trailer has surge brakes which I am told are mechanical not electrical meaning we could cut off the 6 pin round and splice a 4 pin flat. The person who sold us the trailer (private sale) said that it is illegal to downgrade trailer wiring perhaps rendering a feature on the trailer useless and possibly void our insurance. I'm confused, should I add an additional 6 pin round to the car, replace the existing 4 pin with a round pin, or change the 6 pin on the trailer to a 4 pin. I don't really want to lose the 4 pin on the car because most things you tow have that connector.
 
10/07/2010, 04:29 PM Re: 4 pin flat or 6 pin round
Travis ~ Hitch Guy The simplest way to solve this issue is to purchase a 4-6way round adapter. CURT offers a product that simply allows you to to plug your vehicles 4-flat adapter into the CURT 57626 http://tinyurl.com/6wayadptr . Then you can use your 4-flat or 6-way when needed.
